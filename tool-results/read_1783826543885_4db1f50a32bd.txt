   676→ 1introduction to This Book
   677→This book contains informational handouts and worksheets for people learning 
   678→Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) skills. The overall goal of DBT skills training 
   679→is to help you increase your resilience and build a life experienced as worth living. 
   680→DBT skills are aimed at teaching a synthesis of how to change what is and how to accept what is. Skills teach you both how to change unwanted behaviors, emotions, 
   681→thoughts, and events in your life that cause you misery and distress as well as how 
   682→to live in the moment, accepting what is. There are different sets of DBT skills, and no single training program will include all of the handouts and worksheets in this book. Your skills trainer or individual therapist/case manager will direct you to the 
   683→appropriate handouts and worksheets for your particular program.
   684→How This Book Is Organized
   685→There are five main sections in this book, and each begins with a brief introduc -
   686→tion. Following a first section on General Skills, there is a section of handouts and worksheets for each of the four main DBT skills modules: Mindfulness Skills, Inter -
   687→personal Effectiveness Skills, Emotion Regulation Skills, and Distress Tolerance 
   688→Skills. There are topical subsections of handouts and worksheets within each skills module, as described below. Every skill or set of skills has a corresponding handout 
   689→with instructions for practicing that skill. Nearly every handout has at least one 
   690→(often more than one) associated worksheet for recording your practice of the skill. The introductions to each section summarize the handouts, their purposes, and the worksheets that go with them.
   691→General Skills: Orientation and Analyzing Behavior
   692→During Orientation , you will be introduced to DBT and the goals of skills train -
   693→ing, and will be encouraged to identify your own personal goals. You will also be 
   694→oriented to the format, rules, and meeting times of your particular skills program.

   704→Mindfulness Skills
   705→Following a brief presentation on Goals and Definitions , the handouts and work -
   706→sheets for the Mindfulness module focus on Core Mindfulness Skills . These skills 
   707→are central in DBT: They teach how to observe and experience reality as it is, to 
   708→be less judgmental, and to live in the moment with effectiveness. They are the first skills taught, and they support all the other DBT skills. DBT mindfulness skills are translations of meditation practices from Eastern and Western spiritual traditions 
   709→into specific behaviors that you can practice. No spiritual or religious convictions 
   710→are expected or necessary for practicing and mastering these skills.
   711→Other Perspectives on Mindfulness includes several subsets of handouts and 
   712→worksheets. A Spiritual Perspective (including Wise Mind from a Spiritual Perspec -
   713→tive and Practicing Loving Kindness) is a set of handouts and worksheets included for those who consider spirituality an important part of their lives. The skills covered here focus  on experiencing ultimate reality, sensing our intimate connection with the 
   714→entire universe, and developing a sense of freedom. The Skillful Means: Balancing Doing Mind and Being Mind set focuses on balancing two seeming polarities: work -
   715→ing to achieve goals, while at the same time letting go of attachment to achieving goals. The handouts and worksheets for Wise Mind: Walking the Middle Path cover 
   716→skills for finding a synthesis of extremes.
   717→Interpersonal Effectiveness Skills
   718→The handouts and worksheets in the Interpersonal Effectiveness module help you 
   719→manage interpersonal conflicts effectively and maintain and improve relationships 
   720→with other people (those you are close to, as well as strangers). After a short intro -
   721→duction on Goals and Factors That Interfere, there are three main sets of these 
   722→forms. The first set is focused on Obtaining Objectives Skillfully . These are strate -
   723→gies for asking for what you want, saying no to unwanted requests, and doing this 
   724→in a way that maintains your self- respect and keeps others liking you. The handouts 
   725→and worksheets for Building Relationships and Ending Destructive Ones help you 
   726→find potential friends, get people to like you, maintain positive relationships with others, and (when necessary) end destructive relationships. This module’s handouts 
   727→and worksheets for Walking the Middle Path are about walking a middle path in 
   728→your relationships, and balancing acceptance with change in yourself and in your relationships with others.

   732→Emotion Regulation Skills
   733→The handouts and worksheets in the Emotion Regulation module help you to man -
   734→age your emotions, even though complete emotional control cannot be achieved. 
   735→To a certain extent, we all are who we are, and emotionality is part of us; however, 
   736→we can learn to have more control. There are four sets of these forms. The first set covers Understanding and Naming Emotions. Emotions serve important functions, 
   737→and it can be hard to change an emotion if you don’t understand what it does for 
   738→you. The second set covers Changing Emotional Responses. These handouts and 
   739→worksheets help you reduce the intensity of painful or unwanted emotions, such as anger, sadness, shame, and so forth. They also tell you how to change situations that cause painful or unwanted emotions. Reducing Vulnerability to Emotion Mind 
   740→is the third set. The strategies covered here increase your emotional resilience and 
   741→make you less likely to become extremely or painfully emotional . The final set of 
   742→handouts and worksheets deals with Managing Really Difficult Emotions.
   743→Distress Tolerance Skills
   744→The handouts and worksheets in the Distress Tolerance module help you learn to tolerate and survive crisis situations without making things worse. There are two 
   745→main sets of these forms. The Crisis Survival Skills set covers techniques for tolerat -
   746→ing painful events, urges, and emotions when you cannot make things better right 
   747→away. The Reality Acceptance Skills set shows you how to reduce suffering by help -
   748→ing you accept and enter fully into a life even when it is not the life you want. This 
   749→module also includes a set of specialized handouts and worksheets for When the 
   750→Crisis Is Addiction.
